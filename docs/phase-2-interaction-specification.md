## Section 1 — Barry's Response Format Inside RECON

### Problem This Solves

Currently, the section editor gives the user a save confirmation toast and nothing else. Barry is silent after training. There's no signal that the data was understood, no statement of what Barry will do with it, and no preview of the output that the training enables. The RECON experience feels like filling out a form, not tuning an intelligence engine.

The coaching endpoint specified in Phase 1 provides quality classification (`strong / weak / incomplete`) and `confidenceImpact` delta. This section specifies exactly how that data surfaces as Barry's voice in the section editor UI.

### Failure Mode Prevented

Without a visible feedback loop, users who submit weak answers have no way to know their training is thin — they see the same green checkmark as users who wrote specific, detailed responses. The quality system from Phase 1 becomes invisible, and the signal it produces has no place to land in the UX.

---

### The Two-Part Pattern: Mirror + Inference

Barry's response in the section editor is composed of exactly two parts, always in this order, always separated visually:

**Part 1 — Mirror**
Barry paraphrases what he received. This is confirmation, not evaluation. It is written in plain declarative language. No adjectives. No "great," no "I see that," no score. The mirror shows the user that Barry processed the data correctly — and gives them a moment to catch mistakes before they leave the section.

**Part 2 — Inference**
Barry states what he will now assume in every downstream interaction where this section is relevant. These are concrete, first-person operational statements. Not what he "understands" — what he will DO. The number and specificity of inference statements is determined by the `confidenceImpact` delta from the coaching endpoint:

| confidenceImpact | Inference content |
|---|---|
| +10 (strong, high-weight) | 3–4 specific operational statements. Full specificity — references user's actual inputs. |
| +5 (strong, medium-weight) | 2–3 specific statements. Full specificity where fields were completed. |
| +2 (strong, low-weight) | 1–2 statements. Narrowly scoped to what this section contributes. |
| -10 to -15 (weak) | 1–2 statements preceded by a hedge. Explicitly names what Barry CANNOT infer yet. |
| -25 (incomplete, critical) | No Inference block. Replaced with a Gap Warning (see below). |

**Output Preview** (optional third element, visible on demand)
A single tap/click reveals a short excerpt showing what a live Barry output looks like with this section trained. This is a real example — not static placeholder text, but a deterministic, pre-computed sample generated from the user's actual answers by the coaching endpoint and cached in the response. It is shown in the same visual style as an actual Barry output card (muted background, no "AI-generated" badge), because it is generated by the same model.

### Three Worked Examples

#### Example 1 — ICP Section (Section 3: Target Market Firmographics)

**User answers (strong):** Industry: B2B SaaS. Company size: 51–200 employees. Revenue: $1M–$10M. Location: US only.

**Mirror:**
> Your target is US-based B2B SaaS companies, 51–200 employees, $1M–$10M revenue.

**Inference (confidenceImpact: +10):**
> I'll use this to filter Scout results and flag ICP fit. When a contact is at a company in this range, I'll note that in context generation. When they're outside it, I'll flag the deviation without assuming it disqualifies them. I won't assume buying intent from firmographic fit alone — that's your call.

**Output Preview (on-demand, pre-computed from coaching endpoint):**
> *Marcus Reyes is Director of Operations at HealthOps (130 employees, SaaS infrastructure) — inside your target firmographic. Companies at this scale often face the operational complexity your platform addresses, particularly around [product use case from s2 if available].*

---

**Same section, weak answers:** Industry: "software". Company size: not selected. Location: "US."

**Mirror:**
> Your target is US software companies. Size and revenue criteria aren't specified.

**Inference (confidenceImpact: -15):**
> Without size or revenue criteria, I can't distinguish strong ICP matches from weak ones in Scout. I'll note when a contact is in software, but ICP scoring will be broad. Add company size to sharpen this.

*(No Output Preview generated — the coaching endpoint does not produce a preview for weak sections; the preview slot is replaced with the inference statement above.)*

#### Example 2 — Messaging Section (Section 9: Messaging & Value Proposition)

**User answers (strong):** Core value prop: "We cut sales cycle length by 30% through deal health visibility." Tone: direct, not casual. Primary differentiator: native Salesforce integration. Key use cases: pipeline reviews, forecast accuracy, deal coaching.

**Mirror:**
> Your core message is sales cycle reduction through deal visibility. You lead with a 30% cycle reduction claim. Tone: direct. Primary differentiator: native Salesforce integration. Use cases: pipeline reviews, forecast accuracy, deal coaching.

**Inference (confidenceImpact: +10):**
> I'll frame conversation starters around deal visibility and cycle length, not around feature lists. When I suggest outreach, I'll lead with your 30% claim as a specific anchor — not a generic efficiency pitch. I'll stay direct in tone. When a prospect is in a Salesforce shop, I'll reference the native integration proactively.

**Output Preview:**
> *Opening for a VP of Sales at a 150-person SaaS company: "Given how much of sales leadership's time goes into forecast calls — you've probably noticed that visibility into deal health is where things break down. Worth a conversation about what 30% shorter cycles looks like in practice?"*

---

**Same section, incomplete:** Value prop field empty. Differentiator: "we're better." No tone selected.

**No Mirror. No Inference. Gap Warning instead:**

> **Section 9 has required fields without data.**
>
> Barry cannot generate personalized conversation starters or apply your voice to outreach without a value proposition and differentiator. Outreach will use generic category-level framing.
>
> [Complete this section →]

The Gap Warning is not coaching. It is a factual operational statement: here is what Barry cannot do, and here is what to do about it. The link navigates back into the section to the first empty required field.

#### Example 3 — Competitive Positioning (Section 8: Competitive Landscape)

**User answers (strong):** Competitor A: Salesforce (incumbent, complex, expensive). Competitor B: HubSpot (simpler, less enterprise-ready). Competitor C: spreadsheets (internal tool habit). User's positioning against each: speed of implementation, enterprise depth without Salesforce cost, real-time vs. manual tracking.

**Mirror:**
> You compete with Salesforce (incumbent complexity risk), HubSpot (lighter alternative), and spreadsheets (status quo inertia). You position against Salesforce on implementation speed, against HubSpot on enterprise depth, against spreadsheets on real-time visibility.

**Inference (confidenceImpact: +10):**
> When a contact mentions Salesforce, I'll surface your implementation speed advantage — not a feature comparison. When HubSpot comes up, I'll note your enterprise readiness. Against spreadsheets, the frame is cost of manual tracking, not product specs. I'll only surface competitive positioning when the context supports it. I won't lead with competitors unprompted.

**Output Preview:**
> *If a prospect is currently on Salesforce: "A lot of teams at your stage are trying to extend Salesforce rather than replace it — but the implementation overhead of making it do what you need often costs as much as the tool itself. We're built to work alongside it with a 2-week deployment."*

---

**Same section, weak answers:** One competitor named: "Salesforce." No positioning. No other competitors.

**Mirror:**
> You named Salesforce as a competitor. No positioning or other competitors provided.

**Inference (confidenceImpact: -15):**
> I know Salesforce is in your competitive space but not how you position against them. I won't reference competitive context in outreach — I have no basis for doing that accurately. Add positioning detail to unlock this.

### When Barry Goes Silent

Barry's response only appears after a section is saved and the coaching endpoint returns. Barry does not comment on:

- Individual optional fields that are left empty (he only mirrors what IS present)
- Fields that are valid but not relevant to downstream Barry functions (he doesn't acknowledge a company's founding year, for instance)
- Sections that haven't been visited yet in this session

**Barry is silent during data entry.** There is no real-time validation feedback, no character counts from Barry, no encouragement as the user types. Barry speaks once, after save, not during.

---

### When Barry Flags

Flagging occurs at the section level, not the field level. Barry flags when:

1. The coaching endpoint returns `quality: 'incomplete'` AND the section is classified as critical (s1, s2, s3, or s5) — produces a Gap Warning (see Example 2 above)
2. The coaching endpoint returns `quality: 'weak'` for a high-weight section — produces an Inference block that explicitly names what Barry cannot do

Barry never flags with urgency, warnings, or emoji alerts. The flag is a flat operational statement in the same visual style as the Mirror.

---

### What Barry Never Does

- **Compliments.** No "Great answer," "Perfect," "I love that," "Well done." Zero positive reinforcement language.
- **Scores.** Barry never says "this is 80% complete" or "your confidence impact is +10." The `confidenceImpact` number is internal to the Knowledge Map score calculation — it is not surfaced in Barry's coaching text.
- **Fabricates.** Barry's Mirror only paraphrases what the user actually entered. If the user wrote "mid-market SaaS," Barry says "mid-market SaaS" — not "B2B SaaS companies typically operating on $5M–$20M ARR." No hallucinated specifics.
- **Moralizes.** If the data is thin, Barry states what he can't do — not why better data is important, not what the user is missing out on. The Gap Warning contains one factual operational statement and a link. That's it.
- **Writes in first person plural.** "I'll use this" — not "we'll use this." Barry is a single intelligence, not a team.

---

### UI Placement

Barry's Mirror + Inference response appears below the section form, above the "Save" confirmation state, only after a successful save. It is not a modal. It is not a sidebar. It is inline, within the section editor flow, in a visually contained card with a subtle left border in Barry's color (currently purple in the codebase).

The Output Preview (Part 3) is revealed by a "See what Barry says in the field →" link below the Inference block. It does not render by default. The coaching endpoint pre-computes it and includes it in the response payload; the client stores it in component state and renders on demand.

**Component:** `BarryCoachingResponse.jsx` (new component, Phase 2)
**Props:** `{ quality, headline, mirror, inference, outputPreview, confidenceImpact }`

## Section 2 — The Knowledge Map State Machine

### Problem This Solves

The current Knowledge Map in `ReconOverview.jsx` has three states (`trained`, `partial`, `untrained`) and lives as one card in a two-column grid alongside "Train Next." It is a status indicator, not an entry point. The hover tooltip shows a description string and a completion status — not what Barry actually assumes when that dimension is weak.

In v2, the Knowledge Map is the front door to RECON. It is the first thing below the page header. It has five distinct states, each with a specific visual treatment and hover content that tells the user exactly what Barry knows or doesn't know — not just whether the section is completed.

### Failure Mode Prevented

A single-column tile status (trained/untrained) gives the user no reason to improve an answer they've already submitted. Once a section is "trained," the map goes green and they leave. The `weak` state — the most actionable state in the entire system — is invisible in the current design. Without it, the quality system from Phase 1 produces data that has nowhere to go in the UI.

---

### Layout Change: Knowledge Map as Front Door

**Current:** Knowledge Map is a card inside a two-column CSS grid, second row of content, sharing its row with "Train Next."

**v2:** Knowledge Map is the primary content block, full-width, immediately below the page header. "Train Next" moves inside the Knowledge Map as a contextual recommendation panel that appears below the tile grid. The module cards (ICP Intelligence, Messaging, etc.) move below the Knowledge Map.

This is not a cosmetic change — it changes the entry experience. A user who visits `/recon` sees their training state first, before any module cards or platform impact explanations. The score is the first number they see.

---

### Five Visual States

Each tile in the Knowledge Map maps to one visual state. Tiles represent training dimensions (7 dimensions as currently defined in `TRAINING_DIMENSIONS`), not individual sections. A dimension's state is derived from the worst state of its constituent sections.

| State | CSS class | Background | Icon | Label color | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `empty` | `.km-tile--empty` | `#1a1a1a` (dark) | Open circle | `#555` (muted) | No sections in dimension are `completed` |
| `weak` | `.km-tile--weak` | `#2d2200` (dark amber) | Warning triangle (amber) | `#d97706` (amber) | All sections `completed`, at least one `quality: weak`; not stale; no conflict |
| `strong` | `.km-tile--strong` | `#0d2b1a` (dark green) | Check circle (green) | `#10b981` (emerald) | All sections `completed`, all `quality: strong`, not stale, no conflict |
| `stale` | `.km-tile--stale` | `#1a1f2b` (blue-gray) | Clock (slate) | `#64748b` (slate) | Any `completedAt` > 90 days, regardless of quality |
| `conflict` | `.km-tile--conflict` | `#2d1800` (dark orange) | Alert diamond (orange) | `#ea580c` (orange) | ICP drift detected (applies to `icp` dimension only) |

**State priority when multiple conditions are true:** conflict > stale > weak > empty. `strong` only renders when no other condition applies.

**Mobile:** Tiles are 2-column grid on mobile (currently 7 tiles = 4 rows with one full-width). Dimensions are labeled below their icon. Touch target minimum: 52×52px.

---

### State Transition Triggers

| Transition | Trigger | Actor |
|---|---|---|
| any → `strong` | Section saved, coaching returns `quality: strong`, `completedAt` < 90 days, no ICP conflict | `barry-coach-section` response |
| any → `weak` | Section saved, coaching returns `quality: weak` | `barry-coach-section` response |
| strong/weak → `stale` | `computeReconHealth()` reads `completedAt` > 90 days at page load | `computeReconHealth()` |
| stale → strong/weak | Section re-saved (new `completedAt` written) | Save event |
| icp-dimension → `conflict` | `computeReconHealth()` detects zero-overlap between current ICP and `icpSnapshotAtLastReconSave` | `computeReconHealth()` |
| conflict → strong | Section 3 re-saved AND `icpSnapshotAtLastReconSave` refreshed | Section 3 save event |
| any → `empty` | Section reset to `pending` (user-initiated, admin-initiated) | Explicit reset action |

Transitions triggered by `barry-coach-section` response update the tile state optimistically on the client before Firestore is written. The Firestore write confirms the state; if it fails, the tile reverts. This prevents a perceptible delay between saving a section and seeing the tile update.

---

### "Barry's Current Confidence" Display

**What it is:** The `weightedScore` from `computeReconHealth()` — a 0–100 numeric score, not a categorical label.

**Current implementation to replace:** `barryConfidence = overallProgress >= 80 ? 'High' : overallProgress >= 40 ? 'Medium' : 'Low'` (line 287 in `ReconOverview.jsx`). This is unweighted and categorical. Replace entirely.

**Display specification:**

```
BARRY'S CONTEXT CONFIDENCE
[Score]  [Color indicator]  [Δ change from last save]

Example:
  72     ●  amber           ↑ +8 since last update
```

- **Score:** Integer 0–100. Font: monospace or tabular-figures. Size: prominent (24–32px depending on viewport).
- **Color:** Red (0–39), amber (40–64), green (65–84), deep green / emerald (85–100). These map to the threshold colors used in the live output preview (Section 3).
- **Delta:** The difference in score from the previous page load. Shows after any section save that changes the score. Positive delta: green text, upward arrow. Negative delta: red text, downward arrow (can happen if a stale flag appears). Disappears after 5 seconds.
- **Animation:** CSS counter animation on score change — the numeric value counts from old to new over 400ms using a linear easing. The color transitions via `transition: color 300ms ease`.

**Location:** Immediately above the Knowledge Map tile grid. Not in a KPI card alongside other stats. This is the headline number of the entire page.

**What happens to the existing Training Status header:**
The `Training Status` section with three KPI stats (Overall %, Barry Confidence, Sections X/10) is collapsed into the single `contextConfidenceScore` display above the tile grid. "Sections X/10" moves to a smaller secondary line below the score: `8 of 10 sections complete`. The unweighted `overallProgress` percentage is no longer the primary metric displayed.

These are hardcoded strings per dimension. They represent what Barry literally uses when a dimension is weak — not a warning, a factual statement of current behavior.

---

### "Train Next" Recommendation Panel

In v2, this panel appears below the tile grid, inside the Knowledge Map container. It is not a separate card.

**Recommendation logic (updated from current):**
1. If any tile is `conflict`: recommend reconciling the ICP conflict first (navigate to s3 and ICP Settings)
2. Else if any critical tile (s1, s2, s3, or s5) is `empty` or `incomplete`: recommend that section
3. Else if any critical tile is `weak`: recommend the weakest critical section (lowest `confidenceImpact`)
4. Else if any tile is `stale`: recommend the stalest section (oldest `completedAt`)
5. Else if any tile is `weak`: recommend the weakest non-critical section
6. Else if all strong: show the "Fully Trained" complete state

**Display:**
One recommendation at a time, with a clear reason why: "Start here → Business Identity — Barry has no context about what you do or who you sell to." The reason is the `impactWhenMissing` string from `TRAINING_DIMENSIONS`, not generic text.

## Section 3 — The Live Output Preview

### Problem This Solves

`ImpactPreviewPanel.jsx` currently shows three static before/after text examples regardless of what the user has actually trained. A user who has completed zero sections sees the same "RECON-Enhanced Output" example as a user who has completed eight sections. The panel is illustrative, not live.

In v2, the output preview is dynamic. It shows a real excerpt of what Barry would say at the user's current confidence level, and it updates — not in real time as they type, but after each section save changes the weighted score. Users can see their training translate into tangible output changes, section by section.

### Failure Mode Prevented

If the preview never changes, it stops being a signal. Users who've seen it once learn to ignore it. A static preview doesn't teach users what better training produces — it only shows them a marketing example. The dynamic preview is the primary mechanism for demonstrating that quality matters, not just completion.

---

### First-Time User Experience (Zero Context)

When a user visits `/recon` for the first time (all 10 sections are `pending`):

**Knowledge Map:** All seven tiles in `empty` state. Dark background. Muted labels. Confidence score: 0 in red. Delta: no delta shown (first visit).

**Preview panel:** Shows the 0–39% threshold preview by default, using a sample hypothetical contact (Marcus Reyes / Director of Operations / HealthOps — the same persona used in the Phase 1 spec's ImpactPreviewPanel examples). This is a static sample, not a live Barry call. Its role is to establish the preview structure — what slots exist, what unlocks each one.

Below the sample preview:
> *This is what Barry says about a contact right now, with no training. The greyed sections show what becomes available as you train.*

Single CTA: **"Start training →"** — navigates to `/recon/section/1` (Business Foundation). Not to a module page. Directly into the first section. The fastest path from zero to some context is a single click.

---

### Returning User with Partial Context

When a user returns with some sections completed:

**Knowledge Map:** Mix of states based on `computeReconHealth()`. Confidence score in appropriate color. Delta shown if this session's score is different from the last-stored score.

**Preview panel:** Shows the real threshold-state preview for their current `contextConfidenceScore`. If score is 62, they see the "Basic orientation" (40–64%) preview. The greyed slots show exactly what's missing and which section unlocks each one.

The preview uses the **same hypothetical contact** (consistent persona) across all threshold states so users can see the delta between what Barry says now vs. what he'll say after training. Changing the persona between thresholds would make the improvement invisible.

### Preview Update Behavior

The preview does not call the Claude API on each section save. It is a **threshold-gated static preview** — the displayed content changes when `contextConfidenceScore` crosses a threshold boundary (0→40, 40→65, 65→85, 85→95, 95→100).

Within a threshold band, the preview text does not change as the score moves (e.g., from 62 to 67). When the score crosses into the next band, the preview card transitions via a fade animation (200ms `opacity` transition) to the new threshold's content.

The hypothetical contact used for all threshold previews is hardcoded as a constant (`PREVIEW_PERSONA`) in `src/components/recon/KnowledgeMap.jsx`. This contact data is never fetched from Firestore and never personalized to the user's actual contacts — it is always the same sample to enable visual delta comparison across threshold levels.

---

### Threshold Progress Indicator

Below the preview card, a five-step indicator shows the user where they are in the confidence progression:

```
○ — ● — ● — ○ — ○
Not enough  Basic  ICP-aware  Capable  Full
```

Current threshold is filled. Previous thresholds are filled (achieved). Future thresholds are empty (not yet reached). This is a navigation indicator, not a completion bar — tapping a filled node scrolls to show that threshold's preview, so users can see what their output looked like at earlier levels.

### Mobile-First Layout Constraints

**Knowledge Map tiles:** 2 columns on mobile (currently 7 tiles; layout produces 4 rows with the 7th tile full-width or in a 3-column final row — use 3×2 + 1 full-width layout for cleaner mobile presentation).

**Barry's Confidence score:** Large, centered, above the tile grid on mobile. The delta annotation (`↑ +8`) appears inline to the right of the score number, not below it.

**Preview panel:** Full-width on mobile. The greyed-out "unlockable" slots stack vertically. The threshold progress indicator uses icons only (not labels) on narrow viewports.

**Train Next recommendation:** On mobile, this appears as a bottom-anchored persistent strip (not a panel inside the map), so it's visible without scrolling past the tile grid. On desktop, it appears below the tiles within the map container.
